COMM 2084: Media and society

Professor Mackay


Media and society is a course that takes a deeper look at how media is used in our society. In order to do this, we take a look at how issues come about in media today, as well as the cultural issues that influence the media across the globe. This course helps us realize how much our life is impacted by the media around us and within our lives.


Professor Mackay received her degree from UNC Chapel Hill and then completed her masters at University of Alabama. Professor Mackay has been a professor at Virginia Tech since August of 2008. Prior to becoming a professor, she was a reporter for Roanoke Times, Asheville-Citizen Times, Hickory Daily Record, Chapel Hill Herald and a videographer for News Channel 11. She specializes in teaching communication ethics, media instituations, social media theories and practices, communication ethics, multimedia reporting and multimedia journalism. She has a passion for the media and how it impacts people in our society. Outside of her life here at Virginia Tech she takes care of her cats she has at home. There is nothing more important to her than her cats as she would say in the first day of class when going over the syllabus. Professor Mackay is all about making sure students are interacting with one another all of class because everything involves everyones different perspective in this type of course.


This class is mandatory for my major and it is very interesting. The exams can get tricky because the amount of material on each one but it just takes time at home. You must make sure you are keeping up using the textbook. During class she goes over powerpoint slides, but it is up to you to read the necessary chapters outside of class. I really enjoy going to this class. I may not agree with everything that is said, but thats the beauty it is good to hear about things from other peoples perspective. Professor Mackay does a good job teaching this class and giving real life situations and events to explain certain topics.
