TA 2014: Intro to Theatre

Professor Rinehart


Introduction to Threatre is a course that teaches students the basics about each different types of theatre. On top of learning about theatre, students also get to read numerous different plays and analyze them to better understand the content within play. It is fun to see all the necessary components needed to bring theatre production to life. We get to learn about the authors and the history behind each play that we read. Movies are also shown in class so the students can see a different perspective between the play and the play as a movie.


Professor Rinehart grew up in Williamstown Massachusettes before attending UNC Chapel Hill. Professor Rinehart always has had a love for performing and theatre, but after graduating from UNC she found she also had a huge passion for teaching. Now, here at Virginia Tech, she can do both. She can teach other students about the shared passions she has with the numerous plays and performances and aspects of theatre that are taught in her classes. Professor Rinehart is a super fun teacher who is always putting a smile on her students faces. Her classes are never boring because she is always full of life and very expressive during her lectures. Professor Rinehart said that her favorite place on Virginia Tech's campus is the Stuido Theatre. She has said that her favorite thing about teaching is watching students succeed and having a drive to ask questions and want to learn. professor Rinehart has been around theatre since she first started performing at age four. Then, at age five, she was part of the cast for "The Wizard of Oz". Following this performance, there has never been a year of her life where she did not make theatre. A big unknown fact about professor Rinehart is that she is a very big sports fan.


I actually signed up for this class because my friend took it and told me the teacher was very fun. The other reason I took it is because I used to be involved with theatre and constantly in big shows and performances. I needed another Area 6 class and I knew this would be a good one to chose to fulfill that credit. I highly recommend this course to any students. You do not have to be involved with theatre to take this class. It is nice to have a class where I can read plays and watch movies and live performances and learn what it takes to produce these fantastic pieces of theatre. This class is not a big workload. If you commit to doing all the assigned readings for outside of class, it makes going to class more enaging and interactive.
